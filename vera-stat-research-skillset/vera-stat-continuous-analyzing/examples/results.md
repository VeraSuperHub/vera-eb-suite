## Results

OJ supplementation (M = 20.66, SD = 6.61) produced longer teeth than VC supplementation (M = 16.96, SD = 8.27), t(55.3) = 1.915, p = 0.061, Cohen's d = 0.495.

Dose differences were strong, F(2, 57) = 67.42, p < .001, eta-squared = 0.703. Within supplement strata, dose remained positively associated with tooth length in both groups. The interaction test supported significant heterogeneity by supplement.

The OLS model accounted for 73.0% of the variance in tooth length. Across OLS, quantile regression, random forest, and GBM, dose emerged as the dominant predictor, followed by supp_vc.
