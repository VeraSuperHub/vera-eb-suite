## Results

The linear latent growth model converged successfully in N = 400. Global fit was good, chi-square(5) = 8.07, p = 0.152, CFI = 0.998, TLI = 0.998, RMSEA = 0.039 (90% CI [0.000, 0.087]), and SRMR = 0.012. The latent intercept mean was 0.615 (p < .001), and the latent slope mean was 1.006 (p < .001), indicating positive average change over time. Intercept variance (1.932, p < .001) and slope variance (0.587, p < .001) were both statistically different from zero, supporting between-person heterogeneity in baseline status and rate of change. The intercept-slope covariance was 0.618 (p < .001), indicating that participants with higher baseline scores tended to show steeper increases.
