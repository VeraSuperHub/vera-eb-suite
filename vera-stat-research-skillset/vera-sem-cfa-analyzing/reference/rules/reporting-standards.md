# CFA Reporting Standards

1. Report sample size, estimator, `chi-square(df)`, `CFI`, `TLI`, `RMSEA` with CI, and `SRMR`
2. Report standardized loadings for every retained indicator and state whether they are statistically different from zero
3. Describe fit as good, adequate, or suboptimal; do not claim the model is "valid" from fit indices alone
4. Reliability must be tied to constructs, not the overall model
5. Interfactor correlations should be discussed as overlap evidence, not as proof of discriminant validity
6. Discriminant validity claims require explicit evidence (HTMT, Fornell-Larcker, or theory)
7. Modification indices are exploratory; never present them as confirmatory evidence
8. Invariance claims must state the comparison rule used (for example, ΔCFI <= .010)
