## Results

### Distribution and Group Differences

The break-count outcome had mean 28.15 and variance 174.20, yielding a variance-to-mean ratio of 6.19. Observed zeros comprised 0.0% of the sample compared with 0.0% expected under a Poisson model. The Cameron-Trivedi overdispersion test estimated alpha = 0.1116, p < .001, supporting overdispersion and favoring negative binomial modeling.

Break counts differed across tension levels (Kruskal-Wallis H(2) = 10.81, p = 0.004). Mean counts were highest under low tension and lowest under high tension.

### Count Models

Among the count-number models, the NegBin specification had the lowest AIC (475.50). In that model, tension_H showed the strongest standardized association with break counts (IRR = 0.600, 95% CI [0.309, 1.168], p = 0.133).

### Count-Rate Demonstration

With exposure included through offset = log(hours), the Negative Binomial rate model provided the better fit (AIC = 484.95). The likelihood-ratio comparison between Poisson and negative binomial rate models yielded chi-sq(1) = 277.03, p < .001. Rate summaries by tension level are reported in the accompanying table.

### Cross-Method Synthesis

The unified importance table showed that tension_H ranked highest across the best-fitting count model and the tree-based exploratory models. This convergence suggests that the main signal is stable across both parametric count assumptions and nonparametric analytic lenses.
