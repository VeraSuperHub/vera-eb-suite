## Results

### Overall Effect

The meta-analysis synthesized k = 12 studies with a total sample size of
N = 1138. The random-effects model (REML) yielded a pooled standardized
mean difference of g = -0.66, 95% CI [-0.84,
-0.48], z = -7.35, p < .001,
indicating a statistically significant
negative effect favoring the
treatment group. The fixed-effects
model produced a similar estimate (g = -0.66, 95% CI
[-0.78, -0.54]). The 95% prediction interval
[-1.20, -0.12] suggests that effects in individual study contexts
may vary considerably.

### Heterogeneity

Cochran's Q(11) = 3.24, p = .987; I-squared = 0.0%;
tau-squared = 0.0510. Limited
between-study heterogeneity was observed, with
the random-effects framework employed as a conservative approach.

### Publication Bias

Visual inspection of the funnel plot revealed
no marked asymmetry (see Figure 3).
Egger's regression test did not indicate
significant funnel plot asymmetry (intercept = 0.78,
SE = 1.41, p = .594). Begg's rank correlation test
yielded Kendall's tau = 0.24, p = .311.
The trim-and-fill procedure estimated 0 missing
studies,
suggesting no imputation was necessary.
Rosenthal's fail-safe N was 350, exceeding
the 5k + 10 = 70 threshold.

### Sensitivity Analysis

Leave-one-out analysis demonstrated that the pooled estimate was stable, with
effects ranging from -0.675 to -0.641
when individual studies were removed (see Table S2).
No study was flagged as influential based on Cook's distance
(threshold: 4/k = 0.333) or |DFBETAS| > 1.
Cumulative meta-analysis ordered by publication year showed the pooled estimate
converging,
suggesting the evidence base has stabilized.

### Subgroup Analysis

Subgroup analysis by intervention type revealed
no significant difference
between subgroups (Q_between(1) = 2.13,
p = .145). The CBT subgroup (k = 7) yielded a pooled effect of g = -0.73, 95% CI [-1.02, -0.45], I-squared = 0.0%. The Medication subgroup (k = 5) yielded a pooled effect of g = -0.55, 95% CI [-0.94, -0.15], I-squared = 0.0%. 

### Meta-Regression

Mean participant age was a significant
moderator of treatment effect (B = -0.01, SE = 0.00,
p = .010, R-squared = 1.00). Residual heterogeneity
remained non-significant
(QE(10) = 0.77, p = .1000),
suggesting that the model adequately captured between-study variation.

### Cross-Method Comparison

All estimation methods yielded pooled effects in the
same direction, ranging from
-0.66 to
-0.66
(see Table S5). The DL and REML tau-squared estimates were
0.0000 and 0.0510, respectively. The
Knapp-Hartung adjustment narrowed
the confidence interval by 0.20 units
(a material change).
The approximate Bayesian posterior (mean = -0.66, 95% CrI
[-0.79, -0.54]) was consistent with the frequentist REML
estimate. Overall, the main finding appears robust across analytic choices.
