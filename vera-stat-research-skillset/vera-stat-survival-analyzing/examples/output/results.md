## Results

### Follow-Up and Censoring

The complete-case analysis included 168 patients, of whom 121 (72.0%) experienced the event and 47 (28.0%) were censored. Overall median survival was 320 days.

### Kaplan-Meier and Log-Rank Analysis

Survival differed by sex in the primary comparison, with a log-rank test of chi-sq(1) = 6.16, p = 0.013. Median survival was 284 days for male patients and 426 days for female patients.

### Multivariable Cox Proportional Hazards Model

In the adjusted Cox model, ph.ecog was the strongest predictor (HR = 2.08, 95% CI [1.35, 3.23], p = 0.001). The model's concordance index was 0.651. The proportional hazards assumption showed evidence of violation for meal.cal, so the Cox estimates should be interpreted alongside the AFT models.

### Accelerated Failure Time Models

Within the AFT family, the Weibull model provided the best distributional fit (AIC = 1684.8). These models offered time-ratio interpretations that complemented the hazard-ratio results from the Cox model.

### Random Survival Forest and Cross-Method Synthesis

The random survival forest achieved an in-sample concordance index of 0.830, and pat.karno ranked highest by permutation importance. The unified importance table showed how the semiparametric Cox model and the nonparametric survival forest converged on the most influential predictors.
